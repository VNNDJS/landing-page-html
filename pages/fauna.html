FAUNA

Madagascar’s fauna is incredibly diverse and unique, with around 90% of its wildlife being endemic. The island is famous for its lemurs, such as the ring-tailed lemur and the indri, which are found nowhere else on Earth. Other notable animals include the fossa, a carnivorous mammal resembling a small cougar, and the aye-aye, a nocturnal primate with a distinctive long middle finger used for foraging.

Madagascar is also home to a variety of reptiles, including the smallest chameleon, Brookesia nana, and numerous species of geckos and snakes. The island’s isolation has allowed these species to evolve independently, resulting in a rich and unique ecosystem
